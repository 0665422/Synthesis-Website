Analysis: New York Post

In this section we will analyze “Trump-Ukraine story is what you get when the media imagines the facts” editorial published in New York Post. This editorial will revieal anti-impeachment view.

Summary

This article titled “Trump-Ukraine story is what you get when the media imagines the facts” was published September 25, 2019, to the New York Post Website. In the article the writers talk about the incident between the President of Ukraine Volodymyr Zelensky and President Trump that is known as “Ukraine-Gate.” The article specifically talks about the phone call transcript between the two presidents and the media’s reaction to it. In the article the writers claim that its been a complete overreaction by the media and that we shouldn’t be convicting President Trump, especially when we still don’t have all the information. They write in the article that the media is trying to paint a picture of an “out-of-control prez.” What they’re trying to say is that the general public shouldn’t overreact as well, we should wait till we have all the facts, and that some of the things Trump is being accused of were never said according to the Transcript.

Rhetorical Critique

Logos

The New York Post citing all these publications articles is also an example of Logos because they use the logic that “If these four publications that we just linked in our article are overreacting to the news, then all of them are.” Another example of logos in the article is when they discredit the media by saying that they “relied on leaks and third- or fourth-party accounts — not even hearsay, but gossip.”

Pathos

Pathos is used throughout the article to appeal to readers emotionally, in the article they paint a picture of the media being the offender and the President Trump a victim. In the article they make sure to further this point by making it look like the media is calling President Trump an “out-of-control prez.”

Ethos

Throughout the article they make sure to cite multiple writing publications that have already prosecuted President Trump. Citing these publications is an example of ethos because the New York Post being able to back up they’re main claim of the media overreacting to the situation shows credibility.

Idea Critique

In this article published to the New York Post there are some takes that I do agree on but for the most part I mainly disagree with the article. It is true that the media does like to overreact to situations surrounding the president and sometimes it does look like they’re trying to paint a “out-of-control” president. However what President Trump decided to ask the Ukrainian president to do is seemingly brushed off as something that isn’t a big deal. The president of the United States was trying to bribe a foreign leader with $400 million dollars in aid to target a presidential candidate. Then the writers of the article try to excuse his behavior by pointing out another inside in which Joe Biden also threatened Ukraine officials, like if him doing something bad cancels out whatever President Trump is tried to do.
